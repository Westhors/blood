Hypertrygliceridemia

It is a condition of high levels of triglycerides in the blood (hypertrygliceridemia), which increases the risk of heart disease, and may constitute a symptom (Symptom) of a metabolic syndrome. Triglyceride is the primary form of energy storage in the body, in other words: it is a type of fat found in the blood that is a source of energy for the body. In a healthy person, levels of the triglycerides remain normal and balanced.

Christmas disease

It is a type of hemophilia, a blood clotting disease in which sufferers are prone to bleeding. The coagulation system relies on a number of vital proteins (factors) that act on top of the other, in sequence. A deficiency in one of these proteins may cause chain disruption and lead to bleeding. Normal hemophilia (Type A) arises from a deficiency of Factor 8 (VIII). As for Christmas disease (hemophilia type B), it arises as a result of deficiency of factor 9 (IX).

Retinitis pigmentosa

More than a hundred years ago, a disease appeared as patches of discoloration (pigment) at the base of the eye, which were seen using an ophthalmoscope. The common belief was that the conversation was about inflammation. During the twentieth century, it became clear that the disease is hereditary and caused by a genetic defect. It has also been shown that the disease progresses to blindness with age, more than any other genetic disease. There is a group of mutated genes that may cause disease, but only a fraction of them, fewer than ten, are known to this day. The forms of genetic transmission are varied as they may be autosomal dominant, when one of the parents has the disease, or autosomal recessive, when both parents are healthy, but carry the mutated gene, or they may be linked to sex, when the mother is fully She is healthy, but passes the disease to her male children.
